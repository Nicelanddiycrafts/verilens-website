Values

Why we built VeriLens

Product visuals heavily influence buying decisions, but generative AI makes it increasingly difficult to tell what’s authentic. VeriLens was built to give users immediate, understandable signals — directly while they shop — without requiring technical knowledge.

Team

Nysa — Technical lead (detection pipeline, backend, extension logic)

Porsche — Design & media (UI/UX, visuals, demos)

We met through the Crimson research matching system and built a working MVP through iterative testing on real listings.

Model

What happens when a shopper clicks Scan listing.

1

Capture

Locate visible product media on the listing page.

2

Analyze signals

Run lightweight checks: quality, compression, boundaries, multi-region consistency.

3

Score + explain

Combine signals into a risk score with plain-language reasons.

4

Highlight

Show a heatmap indicating where anomalies concentrate.

Feasibility & Potential Impact

Built as a practical MVP first — then refined through real-world testing.

Feasibility

  • Working MVP browser extension that runs on live listings
  • Lightweight CPU-based checks for low latency
  • Explainable outputs: score, reasons, and heatmap visualization

Impact

  • Reduce misleading purchases and shopping frustration
  • Improve consumer confidence at decision time
  • Promote transparency as AI-generated product media grows

How we measure success

  • Detection quality: precision, recall, false-positive rate
  • Product health: downloads, retention, churn
  • User trust: feedback ratings, clarity, Net Promoter Score

Roadmap (responsible growth)

  • Broader testing across categories and platforms
  • Refine explanation clarity and heatmap legibility
  • Optionally add vision-language models for harder cases (selectively)

Brand

Name, voice, design choices, and the identity behind VeriLens.

Calm, evidence-based, and user-first. VeriLens avoids alarmist language and emphasizes uncertainty appropriately.

  • Colors: purple + navy + white (your palette)
